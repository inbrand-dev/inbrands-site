1. Overview

InTrack is a personal habit and behavior tracking app for Android. It allows users to create trackers, log progress, view streaks, review completion rates, use calendar history, set reminders, import and export backups, and optionally protect the app with a PIN.

InTrack is designed to work locally on your device. The app does not require an account, does not use cloud storage, does not run a server, and does not transmit your habit or tracking data off your device.

2. Information InTrack Stores

InTrack stores the information you enter into the app, including:

  • Tracker names
  • Tracker settings and goals
  • Habit or behavior log entries
  • Notes entered into logs, if any
  • Streak and completion history calculated from your logs
  • Calendar history
  • Frozen tracker status
  • App preferences
  • Optional PIN lock setting
  • Notification reminder settings
  • Export/import backup data when you choose to create or restore a backup

This information is stored locally on your device using Android local storage, including a Room database and SharedPreferences.

3. Information InTrack Collects or Transmits

InTrack does not collect, transmit, sell, or share your personal data.

InTrack does not collect or transmit:

  • Name
  • Email address
  • Phone number
  • Location
  • Contacts
  • Photos or videos
  • Camera data
  • Microphone data
  • Health data
  • Financial information
  • Advertising identifiers
  • Device identifiers for tracking
  • Analytics data
  • Crash analytics
  • Usage analytics

4. Local-Only Data Storage

All data created in InTrack remains on your device unless you personally choose to export it.

If you use the export feature, InTrack creates a JSON backup file containing your tracker and log data. You are responsible for where you save, send, upload, or store that exported file.

If you import a backup file, InTrack reads the file you select and restores the data into the app’s local database.

5. Notifications

InTrack may use local Android notifications to remind you about trackers or habits.

These notifications are generated locally on your device. InTrack does not send notification data to any external server.

6. Optional PIN Lock

InTrack may allow you to enable a PIN lock to help restrict casual access to the app on your device.

The PIN setting is stored locally. InTrack does not send your PIN to any server. You are responsible for keeping your device secure.

7. Third-Party Services

InTrack does not use third-party analytics, advertising networks, tracking SDKs, or cloud services.

InTrack does not share user data with third parties.

8. Advertising

InTrack does not display ads.

InTrack does not use advertising identifiers.

InTrack does not track you across apps or websites.

9. In-App Purchases and Donations

The current version of InTrack does not include in-app purchases.

If donation options are added in the future, they may be handled through an external payment provider or platform-supported payment method. Any such payment provider would process payment information according to its own privacy policy. InTrack itself does not store payment card information.

10. Children’s Privacy

InTrack is not specifically directed toward children.

InTrack does not knowingly collect personal information from children. Because InTrack does not transmit user data off device, the app does not knowingly send children’s data to the developer or any third party.

11. Data Deletion

Because InTrack stores data locally on your device, you can delete your data by:

  • Deleting trackers or logs inside the app, if supported
  • Clearing the app’s storage through Android system settings
  • Uninstalling the app

If you created exported backup files, deleting the app will not automatically delete those external backup files. You must delete those files yourself from wherever you saved them.

12. Data Security

InTrack is designed to minimize privacy risk by keeping user data local to your device.

No method of local storage is perfectly secure. You are responsible for protecting your device, device lock screen, exported backup files, and any locations where you choose to store backup data.

13. Changes to This Privacy Policy

This Privacy Policy may be updated from time to time.

If the policy changes, the updated version will be posted at the public privacy policy URL used for the app listing. The effective date will be updated when changes are made.
